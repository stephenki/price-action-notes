# Chapter 1 - Example of How to Trade a Breakout

## 📋 本章摘要

### 要点 1

Many beginners find breakouts difficult to trade because the market moves fast, requiring quick decisions, and often has large bars, which means that there is more risk and traders then have to reduce their position size. However, if a trader learns to identify one that is likely to be successful, the trader's equation can be very strong.

### 要点 2

When a chart discussion runs for multiple pages, remember that you can go to the Wiley website ( www.wiley.com/go/tradingranges ) and either view the chart or print it out, allowing you to read the description in the book without having to repeatedly flip pages back to see the chart.

### 要点 3

Successful breakouts, like the bull breakouts in Figure 1.1 , have excellent math, but can be emotionally very difficult to trade. They happen quickly and traders instinctively know that the risk is to the bottom of the spike (they put their protective stop at one tick below the low of the lowest bar in the bull spike, like below bar 14), which is often more than their usual risk tolerance. They want a pullback but know it will likely not come until the market is higher, and they are afraid to buy at the market because they are buying at the top of a spike. If the market reverses on the next tick, they will have bought at the top of a spike and their protective stop is very far away. However, what they often fail to appreciate is that the math is on their side. Once there is a strong spike in a breakout like this, the probability of at least a measured move up based on the height of the spike is at least 60 percent and may even sometimes be 80 percent. This means that they have at least a 60 percent chance of making at least as much as their initial risk, and if the spike continues to grow after they enter, their risk stays the same but the measured move target gets higher and higher. For example, if traders bought at the close of bar 15 on the 5 minute chart of the 10-Year U.S. Treasury Note Futures in Figure 1.1 , they would risk to one tick (one 64th of a point) below the bottom of the two-bar spike, which is one tick below the bar 14 low, or seven ticks below the entry price. At this point, since the traders believed that the market was always-in long, they thought that there was at least a 60 percent chance of it being higher within a few bars. They should also have assumed that there would be at least a measured move up. Since the spike was six ticks tall, the market had at least a 60 percent chance of trading up at least six ticks before falling to their protective stops.

### 要点 4

At the close of bar 19, the spike had grown to 17 ticks tall and since it was still a breakout spike, there was still at least a 60 percent chance of at least a measured move up. If traders were flat at this point, they could have bought small positions at the market and risked to one tick below the bar 14 bottom of the spike, or 18 ticks, to make 16 ticks (if the market went 17 ticks higher, they could get out with 16 ticks of profit). The traders who bought the bar 15 close were still risking seven ticks to below the bar 14 low, but now had a 60 percent chance of the market trading 17 ticks above the bar 19 high, which would be about a 28-tick profit (14 32nds). The spike ended at bar 19, once the next bar was a bear inside bar instead of another strong bull trend bar. This was the first in a series of pullbacks in the channel phase of the bull trend. Bar 24 went above the measured move target, and the market traded even higher about an hour later.

### 要点 5

In practice, most traders would have tightened their stops as the spike grew, so they would have risked less than what was just discussed. Many traders who bought the bar 15 close might have raised their stops to below bar 17 once it closed because they would not have wanted the market to fall below such a strong bull trend bar. If it did, they would have believed that their premise was wrong and they would not want to risk a larger loss. When bar 19 closed and traders saw that it was a strong bull trend bar, many might have put their protective stops in the micro measuring gap created by the bar 17 breakout. The low of bar 18 was above the high of bar 16, and this gap is a sign of strength. Traders would want the market to continue up and not trade below bar 18 and into the gap, so some traders would have trailed their stop to below the bar 18 low. Many experienced traders use spikes to press their trades. They add to their longs as the spike continues up, because they know that the spike has an exceptional trader's equation and that the great opportunity will be brief. A trader needs to be aggressive when the market is offering trades with strong trader's equations. He also needs to trade little or not at all when it does not, like in a tight trading range.

### 要点 6

The bull spike up from bar 5 flipped the market to always-in long for most traders. When the market traded above the bar 11 wedge bear flag high, it was likely to have approximately a measured move up. Bar 15 was a second consecutive strong bull trend bar and confirmed the breakout in the minds of many traders. Both bars 14 and 15 were strong bull trend bars with good-sized bodies and without significant tails. There was good buying pressure all of the way up from the bar 5 low, as seen by many strong bull trend bars, little overlap, and only a few bear trend bars and the absence of consecutive strong bear trend bars. The market might have been in the early stages of a bull trend, and smart traders were looking for a bull breakout. They were eager to get long once bars 14 and 15 broke out, and traders kept buying relentlessly in pieces all the way up to the bar 19 high.

### 要点 7

The most important thing that traders must force themselves to do, and it is usually difficult, is as soon as they believe that there is a reliable breakout spike, they must take at least a small position. When they feel themselves hoping for a pullback but fearing that it won't come for many more bars, they should assume that the breakout is strong. They must decide where a worst-case protective stop would be, which is usually relatively far away, and use that as their stop. Because the stop will be large, their initial position should be small if they are entering late. Once the market moves in their direction and they can tighten their stop, they can look to add to their position, but should never exceed their normal risk level. When everyone wants a pullback, it usually will not come for a long time. This is because everyone believes that the market will soon be higher but they do not necessarily believe that it will be lower anytime soon. Smart traders know this and therefore they start buying in pieces. Since they have to risk to the bottom of the spike, they buy small. If their risk is three times normal, they will buy only one-third of their usual size to keep their absolute risk within their normal range. When the strong bulls keep buying in small pieces, this buying pressure works against the formation of a pullback. The strong bears see the trend and they, too, believe that the market will soon be higher. If they think it will be higher soon, they will stop looking to short. It does not make sense for them to short if they think that they can short at a better price after a few more bars. So the strong bears are not shorting and the strong bulls are buying in small pieces, in case there is no pullback for a long time.

### 要点 8

What is the result? The market keeps working higher. Since you need to be doing what the smart traders are doing, you need to buy at least a small amount at the market or on a one- or two-tick pullback and risk to the bottom of the spike. Even if the pullback begins on the next tick, the odds are that it won't fall too far before smart bulls see it as value and they buy aggressively. Remember, everyone is waiting to buy a pullback, so when it finally comes it will only be small and not last long. All of those traders who have been waiting to buy will see this as the opportunity that they wanted. The result is that your position will once again be profitable very soon. Once the market goes high enough, you can look to take partial profits or you can look to buy more on a pullback, which will probably be at a price above your original entry. The important point is that as soon as you decide that buying a pullback is a great idea, you should do exactly what the strong bulls are doing and buy at least a small position at the market.

### 要点 9

Some traders like to buy breakouts as the market moves above a prior swing high, entering on a buy stop at one tick above the old high. In general, the reward is greater, the risk is smaller, and the probability of success is higher when entering on pullbacks. If traders buy these breakouts, they usually will then have to hold through a pullback before they can make much profit. It is usually better to buy the pullback than the breakout. For example, rather than buying above bar 6 as the market moved up to bar 9, the trader's equation was probably stronger for a buy above the bar 10 pullback instead.

### 要点 10

The same is true for the bar 11 move above bar 9. With every breakout, traders have to decide if it will succeed or fail. If they believe that it will succeed, they will look to buy the close of the breakout or follow-through bars, at and below the low of the prior bar, and above the high of the prior bar. If they believe that it will fail, they will not buy and if they are long, they will exit their positions. If they believe that the failure will trade down far enough for a scalp, they might go short for a scalp. If they think that the failure will lead to a trend reversal, they might look to swing a short down. In this particular case, as the market moved strongly above the bar 9 and bar 11 double top, it was reasonable to buy the breakout, but traders who bought the close of bar 15 entered around the same price and had a more sound reason to take the trade (buying the close of a strong bull trend bar in a strong bull spike).

## 🎯 核心概念深度解析

### 什么是突破（Breakout）？

**突破**是价格行为（Price Action）中最核心、最常用的交易设置之一。它指的是价格从一个明确的**盘整区间（Trading Range）**或**技术形态**的边界脱离，进入新的价格区域，从而打破原有的供需平衡。

在《Trading Ranges》这本书的语境中，突破特指：
- 从交易区间上沿突破 → 潜在的**多头信号**
- 从交易区间下沿突破 → 潜在的**空头信号**

**突破的构成要素**：
1. **边界明确**：存在清晰的支持位（Support）或阻力位（Resistance）
2. **价格穿越**：一根或多根K线收盘于边界之外
3. **动能确认**：通常伴随成交量放大或K线实体增大

---

### 为什么突破交易对初学者特别困难？

书中原文一针见血：

> Many beginners find breakouts difficult to trade because the market moves fast, requiring quick decisions, and often has large bars, which means that there is more risk and traders then have to reduce their position size.

**三大难点拆解**：

#### 1. 速度压力（Speed Pressure）
- 突破发生时，市场进入**加速阶段**，价格运动迅速
- 留给交易者的**决策窗口极短**（可能只有几秒到几分钟）
- 新手往往在犹豫中错失最佳入场点，或冲动追高被套

#### 2. K线幅度大（Large Bar Size）
- 突破K线通常实体长、波幅大 → 止损距离被迫拉宽
- 根据风险公式：`仓位大小 ∝ 1 / 止损距离`
- **结论**：即使判断正确，单笔盈利可能也被仓位限制而难以大幅盈利

#### 3. 情绪挑战（Emotional Challenge）
- **FOMO**（Fear of Missing Out）：看到价格脱离区间，担心错过整波行情
- **过早入场**：在突破未完全确认时 rush in，陷入假突破陷阱
- **追涨杀跌**：突破后追高 → 回踩止损 → 情绪崩溃 → 报复性交易

---

### 交易者方程（Trader's Equation）在突破中的应用

**交易者方程**是 Brooks 方法论的核心决策框架：

```
(概率₁ × 盈亏比₁) − (概率₂ × 损失比₂) > 0
```

其中：
- 概率₁ = 突破成功、趋势延续的概率
- 盈亏比₁ = 目标盈利 / 风险（止损距离）
- 概率₂ = 突破失败、价格折返的概率
- 损失比₂ = 止损金额 / 风险（恒为 1，因为是固定风险）

**突破交易的期望值计算**：
假设：
- 突破成功率 P_success = 60%
- 失败率 P_fail = 40%
- 盈亏比 R = 2（目标盈利 = 2 × 风险）
- 单笔风险 = $R

期望值 = (0.6 × 2R) − (0.4 × 1R) = 1.2R − 0.4R = **0.8R > 0**

**结论**：只要 `概率 × 盈亏比 > 0.5`，突破交易就从数学上具备正期望值。

**实践意义**：
- 不要交易每一个突破！只选择**概率高 + 盈亏比合理**的设置
- 如果突破K线极大（R 值被迫放大），即使概率高，盈亏比可能恶化 → 放弃或减小仓位
- 回踩入场相比突破入场：概率提升（已确认）但盈亏比下降（入场价更差）→ 需要具体权衡

### 突破的强度维度

Chapter 1 虽未系统讨论，但已暗示关键强度指标：

| 维度 | 强突破特征 | 弱突破特征 |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **K线实体** | 长实体、影线短 | 长影线、小实体 |
| **收盘位置** | 收盘靠近区间顶部/底部 | 收盘在区间中部 |
| **成交量** | 突破时放大、回踩时缩量 | 全程低迷或异常 |
| **后续跟进** | 多根K线连续推进 | 突破后立即停顿或反转 |
| **回踩表现** | 浅回踩、不破突破点 | 深回踩、破位 |

## 📈 价格行为模式详解

### 2.1 突破的标准演化路径（以 Figure 1.1 为例）

基于文本描述和标准突破结构，一次完整的**多头突破**演化可分为三阶段：

#### 阶段 1 — 蓄势（Accumulation / Pre-Breakout）

**市场状态**：
- 价格在支撑区域反复震荡，形成交易区间
- 卖压逐渐枯竭：下影线增多、K线实体变小
- 成交量可能在最后测试时萎缩（供给减少）

**交易者心理**：
- 多头持仓者：套牢但 unwilling to sell at bottom
- 空头持仓者：获利了结，寻找新的做空机会
- 观望者：等待明确方向信号

**识别要点**：
- 震荡区间逐渐收窄（Volatility Compression）
- 出现多次"测试支撑但不破"的K线
- 最后测试的K线可能带有长下影线（ absorption）

---

#### 阶段 2 — 突破瞬间（Breakout Bar）

**K线特征**：
- 一根或多根K线**收盘**于阻力位之上（多头突破）
- 实体长度 ≥ 前期平均K线实体的 1.5–2 倍
- 上影线短（若长上影 → 上方卖压重）
- 理想情况：突破是**连续多根**K线完成，而非单根K线

**成交量**：
- 突破时成交量应**明显放大**（至少 1.5 倍均量）
- 若突破但成交量未放大 → 警惕假突破

**交易者心理**：
- 空头回补（Short Covering）推动突破
- 观望多头开始追入
- 之前的空头可能在突破点尝试做空（失败后回踩）

---

#### 阶段 3 — 回踩确认（Pullback to Breakout Level）

**回踩的本质**：
突破后价格回落至**突破点附近**，测试新形成的支撑是否有效。

**回踩的三种结果**：

| 结果类型 | 描述 | 交易含义 |
|---------|------|----------|
| **Successful Pullback** | 回踩不破突破点，形成支撑 | 二次入场机会，趋势延续 |
| **Failed Pullback** | 回踩跌破突破点 | 突破失败，可能回归区间 |
| **Failure → Opposite** | 回踩至突破点形成反向信号 | 原突破失败，可反向开仓 |

**原文案例解读**：
> The bears who missed the selloff to bar 4, or who took profits at bar 4 and were looking to sell again on a pullback, got their chance at the bar 5 low 2 at the moving average. It had a bear body and was also a 20-gap bar short setup.

**逐句翻译与解析**：
- "bears who missed the selloff to bar 4" → 错过第4根K线下跌的空头
- "took profits at bar 4 and were looking to sell again" → 在bar 4获利了结，寻找再次做空机会
- "bar 5 low 2 at the moving average" → 第5根K线的低点2，恰好位于移动平均线
- "20-gap bar short setup" → 20缺口空头设置（后续章节详述）

**交易启示**：
1. **失败突破可创造反向机会**：即使多头突破"失败"，回踩点可能成为空头的绝佳入场点
2. **位置至上**：bar 5 low 2 的价值在于它位于均线（技术支撑），而非单纯因为它是"回踩"
3. **多时间框架共振**：回踩点同时满足「突破点支撑 + 均线支撑」双重条件

---

### 2.2 突破变体与陷阱

#### 假突破（False Breakout / Fakeout）

**定义**：价格短暂穿越边界，但迅速折返至原区间内。

**典型形态**：
- **长上影线多头假突破**：突破后上冲乏力，收盘大幅回落
- **长下影线空头假突破**：跌破后快速收回，收盘靠近区间顶部
- **跳空缺口假突破**：开盘跳空突破但无持续动能

**防范策略**：
- 等待**收盘价确认**（而非 intraday 突破）
- 观察突破后**首两根K线**的收盘位置
- 检查成交量是否配合

---

#### 突破后立即反转（Failed Breakout → Opposite Trade）

**模式**：突破发生 → 价格回踩 → 在回踩点形成**反向信号** → 趋势反转

**交易逻辑**：
- 突破失败意味着原区间供需平衡未被打破
- 回踩至突破点时，原区间边界重新成为支撑/阻力
- 若出现反向K线组合（如 bear engulfing 后接 bear follow-through），可反向开仓

**风险控制**：
- 反向仓位必须基于**独立的反转信号**，而非"回踩"本身
- 止损设于反向K线的高点（多头）或低点（空头）之外

---

#### 突破后横盘（Breakout → Trading Range）

**模式**：突破成功但随后进入新的交易区间（而非趋势延续）

**处理**：
- 若在突破点入场，可将部分仓位在新区间顶部/底部平仓
- 在新区间内进行高抛低吸，而非追趋势

## 🛠️ 实战操作要点

### 3.1 入场策略对比

| 策略 | 描述 | 概率 | 盈亏比 | 风险等级 | 适合人群 |
|------|------|------|--------|---------|---------|
| **突破入场** | 突破瞬间即入场 | 中 | 高 | ★★★ | 经验丰富、反应快 |
| **回踩入场** | 等待价格回踩突破点/均线 | 高 | 中 | ★★ | 新手首选 |
| **回踩失败反向** | 突破确认失败后反向操作 | 中高 | 中 | ★★☆ | 熟悉反转形态 |

**详细说明**：

**突破入场（Aggressive Entry）**
- 优点：捕获完整趋势，仓位可正常大小
- 缺点：假突破风险高，止损易被触发
- 适用场景：突破K线极强（长实体、高成交量）、趋势市场

**回踩入场（Conservative Entry）**
- 优点：已确认突破有效，假突破风险低
- 缺点：入场价较差（已远离突破点），盈亏比下降
- 关键：回踩必须形成**明确支撑**（如不破突破点、形成小双底、pin bar 等）

**反向入场（Fade Entry）**
- 优点：盈亏比可能极高（反转第一波）
- 缺点：概率相对较低，需精准判断
- 关键：必须有**反转K线组合**确认，而非仅因"回踩"就反向

### 3.2 仓位管理（Position Sizing）详解

**核心公式**：
```
单笔最大风险金额 = 账户总资本 × 单笔风险比例（建议 0.5% – 1%）
每单位合约风险 = (入场价 − 止损价) × 每点价值
仓位大小（手数）= 单笔最大风险金额 / 每单位合约风险
```

**举例说明**：

| 场景 | 账户 | 风险比例 | 最大风险 | 入场价 | 止损价 | 风险点数 | 每点价值 | 每手风险 | 最大手数 |
|------|------|---------|---------|--------|--------|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| 常规 | $100k | 1% | $1,000 | 4500 | 4495 | 5点 | $50 | $250 | 4手 |
| 大K线 | $100k | 1% | $1,000 | 4500 | 4480 | 20点 | $50 | $1,000 | 1手 |

**观察**：突破K线大 → 止损距离从5点扩大到20点 → 每手风险从$250升至$1,000 → 最大仓位从4手降至1手

**进阶策略**：突破入场用1/2正常仓位，回踩确认后再加仓至正常仓位

---

### 3.3 止损设置（Stop Loss Placement）

**三种常用止损位**：

1. **突破点反向止损**（最保守）
   - 多头突破：止损设于突破前盘整区间**底部下方**
   - 优点：极少被正常波动扫损
   - 缺点：距离远 → 仓位小 → 盈亏比恶化

2. **突破K线极值止损**（激进）
   - 多头突破：止损设于突破K线**最低价下方**
   - 优点：止损近 → 仓位可加大
   - 缺点：易被正常波动触发，降低成功率

3. **波动率止损**（ATR-based）
   - 止损距离 = 1.5–2 × 当前ATR值
   - 平衡了市场波动特性与风险控制

**动态止损调整**：
- 突破后形成新支撑 → 将止损上移至新支撑下方
- 趋势发展中 → 使用移动止损（Trailing Stop）保护盈利

### 3.4 出场策略（Profit Taking）

**三阶段出场法**：

| 阶段 | 目标位 | 平仓比例 | 依据 |
|------|-------|---------|------|
| **阶段1** | 等幅运动（Measured Move）<br>≈ 盘整区间高度 | 30–50% | 最小目标，概率最高 |
| **阶段2** | 1.5 × 盘整区间高度 | 再加30% | 强势趋势特征 |
| **阶段3** | 趋势跟踪直至反转信号 | 保留20–40% | 捕捉大趋势尾部 |

**平仓触发条件**：
- 达到预设目标位
- 出现**反转K线组合**（如 bear/bull engulfing, inside bar breakout opposite）
- 价格大幅偏离移动平均线（超买/超卖）
- 交易时段结束（日/周收盘前平仓）

## ⚠️ 常见误区与心理陷阱

### 误区 1：突破就追，不回踩也追

**现象**：
看到价格一突破区间边界就立即追高/追低，不等任何确认。

**后果**：
- 大概率买在**假突破**的高点
- 遇到正常回撤即被止损
- 损失本可避免的风险敞口

**纠正方案**：
- ✅ **等待收盘确认**：突破K线收盘于区间外才视为有效
- ✅ **优先选择回踩入场**：除非趋势极强且无明显回踩
- ✅ **使用限价单**：在预设回踩位挂单，避免情绪化追价

---

### 误区 2：忽略突破K线的"质量"

**现象**：
只看价格突破，不看K线本身的形态特征（实体大小、影线、成交量）。

**后果**：
- 追入**弱势突破**（长上影/长下影） → 高概率假突破
- 无法区分**强突破**与**弱突破** → 交易质量下降

**纠正方案**：
**强势突破K线三大特征**：
1. **实体长**：长度 ≥ 前期平均K线的 1.5–2 倍
2. **影线短**：上/下影线不超过实体的 1/3
3. **收盘在极端**：多头突破 → 收盘靠近最高价；空头突破 → 收盘靠近最低价

**弱突破警惕信号**：
- 长上影 + 小实体（多头） → 上方卖压重
- 长下影 + 小实体（空头） → 下方买盘强
- 突破后成交量未放大 → 动能不足

---

### 误区 3：突破后仓位过重

**原文警告**：
> "...often has large bars, which means that there is more risk and traders then have to reduce their position size."

**典型错误**：
因FOMO（错失恐惧）而使用常规仓位，单次回撤即造成重大账户损失。

**纠正方案**：
- 严格执行**单笔风险上限**（建议 ≤ 1%）
- 突破K线大 → 止损距离宽 → 仓位自动压缩
- 进阶：突破入场用 1/2 仓位，回踩确认再加至正常仓位

---

### 误区 4：回踩时过早反向开仓

**现象**：
价格回踩至突破点，尚未出现反转信号就急于反向开仓。

**后果**：
- 可能错失原趋势的延续（突破成功）
- 反向仓位在趋势延续中被止损

**纠正方案**：
- 反向交易需要**独立的反转K线组合**确认
- 不能仅因"回踩"就认为趋势反转
- 等待：pin bar、 engulfing、inside bar breakout opposite 等信号

---

### 误区 5：忽视更大周期背景

**现象**：
只看当前图表上的突破，不考虑更高时间框架的趋势状态。

**后果**：
- 在日线熊市中做多突破 → 逆大势
- 忽略更大周期的关键支撑/阻力

**纠正方案**：
- **多重时间框架分析**：
  - 日线定大势（Trend Direction）
  - 1小时找突破设置（Setup）
  - 5分钟定具体入场（Entry Timing）
- 突破交易**顺势**（与大周期方向一致）成功率更高

### 突破交易检查清单（Pre-Trade Checklist）

在入场前逐项核对：

- [ ] **边界清晰**：存在明确支撑/阻力位（≥2–3次测试）
- [ ] **突破确认**：K线收盘于区间外（非 intraday）
- [ ] **K线质量**：实体长、影线短、收盘在极端
- [ ] **成交量**：突破时放大（若可用）
- [ ] **仓位计算**：单笔风险 ≤ 1% 账户
- [ ] **止损明确**：已设定止损位置（突破点反向 / 突破K线极值）
- [ ] **目标合理**：至少 1.5 × 止损距离（盈亏比 ≥ 1.5）
- [ ] **时间框架**：与大周期趋势方向一致（若顺势）

### 典型突破结构图示（文字版）

```
Day 1–N: 蓄势阶段
  价格在 [S, R] 区间震荡
  ↓ 卖压减弱（下影线增多）
  ↓ 成交量萎缩

Bar X: 突破K线
  收盘 > R（多头）
  实体长 + 成交量放大

Bar X+1, X+2: 回踩
  价格回落至突破点附近
  测试新支撑（原阻力变支撑）

Scenario A: 回踩成功
  不破突破点 → 形成支撑 → 二次入场 → 趋势延续

Scenario B: 回踩失败
  跌破突破点 → 回归区间 → 止损离场

Scenario C: 回踩反转
  回踩至突破点 + 反向K线组合 → 原趋势失败 → 反向开仓
```
